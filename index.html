02 Research direction

Make human immunity better at fighting bacteria.

My long-term focus is antimicrobial resistance—especially Staphylococcus aureus— approached through immune engineering, gene and cell editing, and computational design.

THE ABSTRACTION PROBLEM

Biology is still too often operated at the equivalent of assembly language.

I want to help build higher-level systems that let researchers design, test, and reason about biological mechanisms more directly—without hiding the underlying science.

01 / PATHOGEN

Staphylococcus aureus

Understand and counter a clinically important pathogen, with antibiotic resistance as the central problem.

02 / HOST

Human immune systems

Improve bacterial detection and targeting, add protective mechanisms, or tune immune responses when necessary.

03 / METHOD

Editing + computation

Explore CRISPR, base editing, engineered immune cells, and machine-learning-guided design as candidate routes.

Current foundation
  • Microbiology laboratories
  • Disc diffusion & MIC
  • PCR & gel electrophoresis
  • Quantitative analysis
  • Python & data workflows

04 About

I build the tool I wish the lab had.

I’m Beny, a Biotechnology & Food Engineering student at the Technion. My best work starts when a scientific workflow feels needlessly difficult: understand the method, make the reasoning visible, then build a tool that respects both.

In a food microbiology lab, a wild-picked rosemary sample produced no microbial growth. Rather than dismissing an empty plate, I started asking whether antimicrobial compounds in the plant could explain it. That reflex—turning an anomaly into a better question—is what pulls me toward microbiology and antimicrobial resistance.
